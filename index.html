What is the capital gearing ratio?
The capital gearing ratio is the ratio of debt that a company has in relation to it's equities. simply the capital gearing ratio is the financial risk of the firm. sometimes it is also called financial leverage or financial gearing. How to calculate capital gearing ratio?
To calculate capital gearing ratio you should use the following formula:
capital gearing ratio = common stockholder's equity / fixed interest bearing debt
example: company x has $430,000 of share holdr's equity, $300,000 in short term debt and $400,00 worth of long term debt, calculate the capital gearing ratio?
Using the capital gearing ratio formula the answer will be:
capital gearing ratio = common stockholder's equity / fixed interest bearing debt
capital gearing ratio = $430,000 / ($300,000 + $400,000)
capital gearing ratio = 0.61
